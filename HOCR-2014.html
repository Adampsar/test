MEDIA


HEAD OF THE CHARLES

October 18, 2014

BOSTON, MA  – On Thursday, October 16 and Friday, October 17, select members of the University of Michigan Men's Rowing Team travelled to Boston, Massachusetts to compete in the prestigious Head of the Charles Regatta. The Charles is the largest two - day regatta in the world, and attracts approximately 300,000 spectators annually. The team raced a Collegiate 4, which competed on Saturday, and a Collegiate 8, which competed on Sunday. An Alumni 8 also raced for Michigan on Saturday.

The first race for the program came in the early afternoon Saturday, with the Alumni 8 racing at 12:38 PM. The crew, which included 2012 Olympian Tom Peszek '07 and 2008 Olympian Matt Hughes '04 in stern pair, finished an impressive fourth place for the second straight year in the 55 boat field. The alumni were 15.43 seconds off of winner Northeastern, but only 0.1 seconds off of third place finisher Crabtree (Cambridge alumni). Besides Peszek and Hughes in stern pair, the boat consisted of Riley Hall '12, Josh Brown '05, Jon Miller '07, Louis Schaljo '14, Nick Sferrazza '14, and Josh Getz '12, and was coxed to a penalty - less row by Remi Taibleson '12.

Later Saturday, the Collegiate 4 of Junior Lucien Gerondeau, Junior Jim Garay, Sophomore Ben Getz, Junior Ben Rebertus, and Sophomore Maddie Boisvert had Bow Number 2 for their 4:00 PM race. Unfortunately, Virginia (Bow Number 1) entered a crew with the same four oarsmen as their gold medal four at the 2013 Charles, and were able to pull out the victory, 25.39 seconds ahead of fourth - place finisher Michigan. However, UM's young crew had the fastest times of the event at the two final checkpoints (from Weld to Cambridge BC and from Cambridge BC to the finish line).

The next day, the Collegiate 8 of Alex Brown, Matt Herbers, Ian McGraw, William Burstein, Wes Vear, Alika Ziker, Daniel Vitas, Mitchell Tyson, and Demi Shanahan tried to ride the momentum of the 4's solid row the day before. The crew executed their 2:52 PM race nearly perfectly, finishing with no penalties in 15:14.73, 8.05 seconds faster than second place Hobart. This marked the first time in program history that a Michigan 8 had won the Head of the Charles.

The crews will rejoin the team in Ann Arbor, where they will continue to train before leaving for the Head of the Hooch in Chattanooga, Tennessee on Friday, October 31
