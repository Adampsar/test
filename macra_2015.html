MEDIA


2015 MACRA Championships

April 25, 2015

YPSILANTI, MI  – On Saturday, April 25, the University of Michigan Men's Rowing Team travelled to Ford Lake in Ypsilanti, Michigan, where they co - hosted the MACRA Championships with Eastern Michigan University. The regatta marked the beginning of championship racing for the squad, who swept the Varsity events and decisively took home the Team Points Trophy.

Racing opened with the heats for the Varsity 4. Michigan's A and B entries both qualified easily, although the latter's race was protested. In the Grand Final, Michigan A won conclusively over Notre Dame, while Michigan B placed fourth. Michigan C placed third in their heat, sending them to the Petite Final, where they placed second.

Varsity racing continued with UM's two Lightweight 4 entries, both of which advanced to the Grand Final. The A entry, racing with the same four oarsmen as the 2014 Lightweight 4 that medalled at the ACRA National Championships, won with a tremendous 15.5 - second margin, while Michigan B finished fifth.

The freshman crews showed marked improvement, with the Freshman 8 cruising to a Grand Final berth with a 24.8 second victory over Minnesota in the heats before winning a silver medal in their afternoon race. The Freshman 4 also defeated Minnesota in their heat before earning a bronze medal in the Grand Final, only 1.2 seconds behind second - place Dayton. Due to unsafe conditions and delays, the Second Freshman 8 and Third Freshman 8's final was cancelled.

In the straight final of the Varsity Pair, Michigan's A entry took the gold over Minnesota, while Michigan B finished fifth. Due to rough conditions, the race was run over a 1000 - meter course. There was also a straight final for the combined event of the Second Varsity 8 and Lightweight 8. UM's Second Varsity won by 6.5 seconds over Mercyhurst, while the Lightweight 8 placed fifth.

In the Varsity 8, Michigan's crew qualified easily before defeating Grand Valley by 4.7 seconds in the Grand Final to win the Ralph Lindamood Cup. This was the fourth time Michigan's Varsity 8 had faced Grand Valley's top crew this season, with two more matchups scheduled at the Dad Vail Regatta and ACRA National Championships.

The team will return to Ann Arbor, where they will continue training before select crews leave for the Dad Vail Regatta in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania on Thursday, May 2.
