Michigan Men's Rowing Welcomes Class of 2018

October 1, 2014

ANN ARBOR, MI  – With the selection process officially completed, the Michigan Men's Rowing Team has officially announced the current freshman class. 39 oarsmen and 8 coxswains were selected, the vast majority having never rowed before coming to UM. Members of the class hail from 10 different states across the country, including California, New York, Pennsylvania, Illinois, Florida, Massachusetts, Connecticut, Minnesota, New Jersey, and (of course) Michigan, as well as Washington, DC. The class also has an international presence, with oarsmen coming from as far as Brazil and Thailand.

The class of 2018 also adds some height to the team, with 13 oarsmen standing 6'3 or taller. A sizeable portion of the class rowed in high school, with athletes coming from programs like Phillips Exeter (NH), Berkeley (CA), and Conestoga (PA), as well as local programs Skyline and Pioneer.

Regarding the squad, Freshman Coach Russell Giacobbe said: "The freshman class looks very promising. We managed to recruit some high - quality athletes as walk - ons, as well as some talented high schools rowers. We look forward to further developing the group over the next few months as we push toward our goal of winning the ACRA National Championship in late May."

The freshman will continue training in Ann Arbor before their inaugural competition at the Head of the Grand this weekend.
