MEDIA


ECAC/NIRC Championship

May 10, 2015

WORCESTER, MA  – On Sunday, May 10, the University of Michigan Men's Rowing Team travelled to Worcester, Massachusetts to race at the ECAC/NIRC Regatta. The squad came away with six Grand Finalist crews and four medals, three of which were gold. This gave Michigan its fifth consecutive Emerson Trophy, which is awarded to the Men's Team Points Champion.

Racing opened with the heats for UM's three Freshman 8 entries. UM's First Freshman 8 advanced to the Grand Final with a second - place finish behind Williams, while the Second Freshman 8 and Third Freshman 8 moved on to the Petite Final by finishing fifth and sixth in their heat. In the Petite Final, the Third Freshman 8 placed third, while the Second Freshman 8 placed fourth. In the Grand Final, the top frosh crew placed fifth, finishing behind Williams, Ithaca, Trinity, and Bucknell.

The varsity squad's day of racing began with the heat for the Third Varsity 8, in which UM's two entries advanced to the Grand Final by placing first and second. In the final, the Third Varsity 8 A won the Grand Final in a time of 6:34.346, while the Third Varsity 8 B placed sixth. 6 - seat of the A - entry, Lucien Gerondeau, was scratched from the lineup before the final due to an oblique injury, forcing Steve Sikora of the Open 4+ to fill his seat. Freshman Peter Tirella sat 2 - seat of the Open 4+ in replacement of Sikora. The 4+ ended up winning the Grand Final in a time of 7:21.646, 5.231 seconds ahead of silver medalist Bucknell.

The Second Varsity 8 advanced to the Grand Final by winning their heat over Worcester Polytechnic Institute. Once in the final, the crew won a silver medal, 5.626 seconds behind winner Hobart. In the Varsity 8, the crew advanced by winning their heat in a time of 6:15.907 before sprinting past Hobart in the Grand Final to give Michigan its sixth Varsity 8 title in ECAC history.

The team will return to Ann Arbor, where they will continue training before leaving for the ACRA National Championship in Gainesville, George on Thursday, May 21.
