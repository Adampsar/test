MEDIA


Dad Vail Regatta

May 8, 2015

PHILADELPHIA, PA  – On Thursday, May 7, select members of the University of Michigan Men's Rowing Team travelled to Philadelphia, Pennsylvania to race at the Aberdeen Dad Vail Regatta. The regatta was the start to a long weekend of racing for the squad, as the crews left Philadelphia after racing Saturday afternoon and drove to Worcester, Massachusetts, where they raced at the ECAC/NIRC Regatta the next day.

Racing opened on Friday morning with a victory for the Varsity 4+ in their heat, advancing them to quarterfinals. That afternoon, the crew placed second to Vermont to move on to semifinals. In a tough semifinal against Colgate (the defending Dad Vail champions in the event), the crew finished fifth and did not advance to the Grand Final.

With a second - place finish to Lafayette, the Varsity 2- advanced to semifinals, where the crew again advanced by placing second, this time to High Point. Once in the Grand Final, Michigan placed fourth behind Western Ontario, High Point, and Lafayette, missing a medal by only 4.631 seconds. UM's other small boat entry, the Open 1X, finished fourth in the heat and did not advance to semifinals.

The regatta was a disappointing showing for the Wolverines' two freshman crews, as neither made the Grand Final. The Freshman 4+ advanced to semifinals with a second - place finish behind SUNY Maritime, but finished third in their semifinal to end their progression. The Freshman 8 placed second to Pittsburgh in their heat before falling to Western Ontario and Virginia in the semifinal.

The Second Varsity 8 advanced to semifinals with a second - place finish behind Virginia in their heat. In the semifinal, the crew progressed to the Grand Final with another second - place finish, this time behind Drexel. In the Grand Final, Michigan placed fourth with a 1.129 second margin behind bronze medalist Virginia.

The Lightweight 4+ advanced to semifinals by winning their heat with a time of 7:03.966. The crew proceeded to advance to the Grand Final with a third - place finish behind Temple and MIT in their semifinal. In the Grand Final, the boat made history by becoming the first Michigan Lightweight 4+ in team history to win a medal at the Dad Vail, placing third behind Temple and Fordham.

The Varsity 8 advanced to semifinals by winning their heat easily. The crew also won their semifinal, finishing in a time of 5:44.924 and drawing Lane 4 for the Grand Final. Looking to win the Richard O'Brien Trophy for the second consecutive year, Michigan led the field for the majority of the race before the Florida Institute of Technology sprinted through the crew to win in a time of 5:37.169, only 0.756 seconds ahead of UM.

All Michigan crews that attended the Dad Vail left for Massachusetts soon after their final race, meeting up with the rest of the squad and competing at the ECAC/NIRC Regatta on Sunday.
