MEDIA


Cincinnati Regatta

April 03, 2015

BETHEL, OH  – On Friday, April 3, the University of Michigan Men's Rowing Team travelled to Bethel, Ohio for the Cincinnati Regatta. Although a dual with Wisconsin was traditionally scheduled for the weekend, a late thaw date and scheduling changes sent to the squad to race on Lake Harsha.

Racing opened in frigid temperatures at 8:00 AM with the heats for the Varsity 8. The Regatta employed an unusual progression system in which the top six times from separate heats advanced to the final, rather than the traditional finishing order system. The 1V controlled the first heat, beating Grand Valley's 1V and UM's 2V, both of whom advanced. Michigan's 3V also qualified for the final by winning the second heat.

In the final, the 1V dominated the race from the start, finishing in a time of 5:48.84, 6.12 seconds ahead of second place Grand Valley. In an upset, Michigan's 3V of senior coxswain Emily Kuret and sophomores Jack Jasper, Colin Darnton, Ben Sonnega, Phil Faris, Scott Griffith, Michael Griffin, Toby Buth, and Ben Getz placed third, beating the Michigan 2V by over four seconds. Grand Valley's 2V finished fifth, with host Cincinnati's 1V finishing sixth.

Most oarsmen from the team's top three 8s raced twice. Three 4s were made from 2V and 3V rowers, all of which qualified for the final and placed third, fourth, and sixth. The 1V broke into four pairs, and made up the entirety of the four - boat final.

The 1F, missing two oarsmen due to illness, advanced to the final with the 2F, where the crews took the top two spots, separated by less than 10 seconds. The frosh also raced in 4s. UM's only straight final of the day was the Open 2X. Michigan's entry of Jacob Dennis and Evan Harris placed fifth.

The team will return to Ann Arbor, where they will continue training before leaving on Friday, April 10 for Lubbers' Cup in Spring Lake, Michigan.
