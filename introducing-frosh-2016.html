Michigan Men's Rowing Welcomes Class of 2020

October 19, 2016

ANN ARBOR, MI  – Each year, Michigan Men's Crew welcomes an incoming class of rowers to our novice squad. Over the course of the season, veteran and new-to-sport athletes become well acquainted with teammates, coaches, equipment, and the overall culture, which define the Michigan rowing experience.

Head Freshman coach Russell Giacobbe had the following to say about this year's class:

This is a group from across the country that represents a deep class of guys composed of athletes from a number of high school rowing programs as well as a large portion of walk-ons. With 47 rowers and 11 coxswains it is the largest class in team history and they have picked up the rowing stroke quickly.

We have rowers from CRI, Exeter, Montclair High School, Huron High School, Northville High School, Pelham Community Rowing, Saline High School, and St. John's High School. This group is joined by a bunch of very talented walk-ons including numerous all-state athletes looking for a new athletic pursuit in college and a chance to compete as a Michigan student-athlete.

Coach Giacobbe's optimism has already found its grounding in an impressive result from the Freshman 8+ and Four+ at the Head of the Rock Regatta in early October.

This week, all novice and some varsity members will travel to East Lansing, Michigan to compete against Michigan State in the Head of the Grand. For many of the novice rowers, this will be their first opportunity to compete with the team.
