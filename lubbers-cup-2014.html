MEDIA

Lubber's Cup 2014

April 12, 2014

ANN ARBOR, MI  – On Saturday, April 12, the University of Michigan Men’s Rowing Team travelled to Spring Lake, Michigan for a weekend of racing at the Lubbers Cup Regatta. The regatta took place a week after another tough weekend of racing for the squad during which the 1V travelled to San Diego to race at the San Diego Crew Classic, while the rest of the team travelled south to scrimmage the University of Virginia.

Racing opened on Saturday morning with the premiere event, the 1V. The crew, made up of Demi Shanahan, Nick Sferrazza, Matthew Herbers, Alex Crawford, Louis Schaljo, Wes Vear, Brandt Rosentretor, Mitchell Tyson, and Philippe Kirschen, was the same lineup that won the petite final a week earlier in San Diego. Racing Purdue and the University of Illinois, the 1V posted a time of 5:58.1 for the victory. That afternoon, the crew won convincingly over Michigan State University and the University of Cincinnati, and on Sunday, they completed the undefeated weekend with a victory over Grand Valley State University, the regatta’s host.

Later Saturday morning, the freshman squad began their racing with the 1F beating Purdue, while the 2F raced against Illinois and Purdue. That afternoon, the freshmen went unbeaten, as the 1F dominated Michigan State and Illinois and the 2F beat the Purdue 3F. On Sunday, the frosh continued their successful weekend with the 2F beating Purdue’s 3F again and the 1F losing a close race with Grand Valley by only 0.3 seconds.

In the fours events, Michigan was again dominant. The A Four and C Four (made up of five freshmen) both beat Purdue Saturday morning, while the B Four lost to Cincinnati. After some lineup switches between races, the A Four remained undefeated by beating Michigan State and Cincinnati that afternoon, while the B Four beat Purdue and Grand Valley and the Freshman 4 beat Colorado State and Cincinnati’s B Four. The next day, the A Four won their race over the B Four and Colorado State, while the freshmen beat Grand Valley and lost a close battle with Cincinnati.

The team’s 3V lost to the 2Vs of Grand Valley and Purdue Saturday morning before being defeated by Michigan’s 2V and Grand Valley again that afternoon. The next day, the boat lost to Purdue’s lightweights and Grand Valley. Unfortunately, the crew had to race other team’s 2Vs, as the regatta did not feature a separate 3V event.

Perhaps the most commanding victories of the regatta came from Michigan’s 2V. The crew won every race by open water, including their matchups with Purdue’s lightweights and Illinois on Saturday morning, their race with Michigan’s 3V and Grand Valley that afternoon, and Sunday’s battle with Purdue and Illinois.

Unsurprisingly, the team left on Sunday having capturing the Men’s Team Points Trophy. The team’s next race will be the MACRA Championships in Ypsilanti, Michigan on April 26.
