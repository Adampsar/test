20th Annual Lubbers Cup Regatta

April 10, 2015

SPRING LAKE, MI  – On Friday, April 10, the University of Michigan Men's Rowing Team travelled to Spring Lake, Michigan for the Lubbers Cup Regatta. The regatta was run in a series of races between two or three boats, with the total margin of victory within a crew over the course of the weekend ultimately determining the champions.

Racing opened on Saturday with the Varsity 8, who beat Michigan State and host Grand Valley. UM trailed the race through the 1000 - meter mark, but surged through GVSU to win by a 1.87 second margin. The 1V's second race of the day, a 6:00 PM matchup with Notre Dame, was more one - sided, with the Wolverines leading wire - to - wire and winning by an 11 second margin. The crew of Demi Shanahan, Alex Brown, Matt Herbers, William Burstein, Mitch Tyson, Alika Ziker, Wes Vear, Ben Rebertus, and Dan Vitas also raced in the final contest of the weekend on Sunday afternoon, in which they defeated Purdue by 18.4 seconds and Michigan State by 26.4 seconds.

In order to use the regatta as a selection tool for the tightly - packed 2V and 3V, the lineups of the crews were switched over the course of the weekend in order for different combinations and personnel to be tried in racing situations. The first 2V of Emily Kuret, Jack Jasper, Colin Darnton, Jordan Shore, Ben Dirkse, Grey Braybrooks, Damian Rotarov, Ben Getz, and James Garay commanded their Saturday morning battle with Grand Valley, as well as UM's 3V and 4V. In the afternoon, Kuret was swapped for sophomore Chelsea Tistle, while junior Ian McGraw replaced Braybrooks in 4 - seat. The result was another victory over the 3V and 4V, as well as Notre Dame.

On Sunday morning, Maddie Boisvert was rotated into the coxswain seat of the 2V, with McGraw remaining in 4 - seat, Jasper shifting from stroke to 2 - seat, Braybrooks moving up to stroke seat, and Getz switching into the 3V. This combination produced the largest margin of victory for the crew, a 7.1 second victory over Purdue. The 3V also won their race against Purdue and Michigan's 4V.

Finally, UM's varsity squad raced three different combinations in 4s, comprised of oarsmen from the 3V and 4V. Sophomore Caroline Gorniak coxed all three crews, the first of which featured all four oarsmen from the Lightweight 4 that medalled at the 2014 ACRA National Championship in Jordan Sykes, Hayden Sonnier, Steve Sikora, and Andrew Barber. William Denman, Jacob Dennis, Evan Harris, and Zach Dowling raced in the second combination, and Ben Sonnega, Phil Faris, Jake Presto, and Toby Buth raced in the final lineup. Sophomore Garrett Klumpar was originally slated to bow the final 4, but was replaced by Buth due to illness.

The freshman also had a productive weekend, with the 1F winning their first race over Michigan State and UM's 2F before the two crews fell to Notre Dame on Saturday afternoon and Purdue on Sunday morning. The 3F won their first race over Cincinnati and Illinois before falling to Michigan State in a close race on Saturday afternoon and Purdue on Sunday morning.

The team will return to Ann Arbor, where they will continue to train before leaving for the MACRA Championship in Ypsilanti, Michigan on Saturday, April 25.
