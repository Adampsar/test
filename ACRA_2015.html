MEDIA


2015 ACRA National Championship

May 23, 2015

GAINESVILLE, GA  – On Thursday, May 21, the University of Michigan Men's Rowing Team travelled to Gainesville, Georgia to compete at the ACRA National Championships. The regatta, which took place at the 1996 Olympic Games rowing course on Lake Sidney Lanier, marked the end of the spring racing season for the Wolverines.

Racing opened on Saturday with the heat for the Varsity 1X. The sculler placed second to Seattle to advance to Sunday's semifinals, where he qualified for the Grand Final by only 0.3 seconds. Once in the Grand Final, the sculler placed seventh.

The team's other small boat, the Varsity 2-, won their heat in a time of 7:18.7 to advance directly to the Grand Final, where they finished fourth behind High Point, Connecticut, and Case Western. The crew was only 3.72 seconds off of winning a medal. The Varsity 4X placed third in their race for lane placement, finishing in a time of 7:17.3. In the Grand Final, the crew placed sixth in a time of 7:15.42.

The freshman squad showed marked improvement at the regatta. The Freshman 4+ placed third in their heat, putting them in the repechage Saturday afternoon. In the repechage, the crew beat Ohio State to advance to the Grand Final, where they finished seventh. The Freshman 8 placed second in their heat to advance to the Grand Final, where they finished sixth. Meanwhile, the Second Freshman 8 placed third in their race for lane placement, finishing in a time of 6:18.8. In the Grand Final, the crew finished fourth behind Orange Coast, UCSB, and Notre Dame. The highlight performance by the freshmen came from the Freshman Lightweight 4+. The crew won their race for lane placement before winning a silver medal in the Grand Final, 3.95 seconds behind first - place Delaware.

The Varsity 4+ placed third in their heat to advance to the semifinal, where they again placed third, 0.5 seconds ahead of fourth - place UCSB and only 0.6 seconds behind second - place Michigan State. The crew moved on to the Grand Final, finishing fifth behind UC - Davis, UCLA, Vermont, and George Mason and missing a medal by only 3.59 seconds. The Second Varsity 4+ won their heat easily to advance to the Grand Final, where they won a bronze medal behind Georgia Tech and Virginia. The crew was only 0.49 seconds off of a silver medal.

The Second Varsity 8 won their heat easily over North Carolina before winning a silver medal in the Grand Final, 2.09 seconds behind first - place Virginia. This was the first time since 2008 that Michigan had not won the event.

The Varsity Lightweight 4+ finished 3.37 seconds ahead of San Diego State in their heat to advance to the Grand Final. In Sunday's race, the crew won a National Championship with a time of 6:42.00, 2.47 seconds ahead of Washington State. The crew retained four of the five members of the 2014 Varsity Lightweight 4+ that won a bronze medal at the National Championships, and was the same crew that won a bronze medal in the event at the Dad Vail.

The Varsity 8 won their heat to advance to Sundays semifinals, which they finished less than two seconds seconds ahead of Grand Valley to advance to the Grand Final. Their time of 5:47.00 was the fastest time between the two semifinals. In the Grand Final, the crew won a National Championship in a time of 5:45.40. The win marked the third consecutive Varsity 8 National Championship for UM, matching the streak held by the team from 2008 - 2010.

Overall, all 12 Michigan crews made the Grand Final, winning five medals, two of which were gold. For the eighth consecutive year, UM came away with the Men's Team Points Trophy. The squad will return to Ann Arbor and begin summer training, with four oarsmen and three members of the coaching staff representing the United States at the World University Games in Gwangju, South Korea from July 3 - 14.
