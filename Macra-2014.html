MEDIA

MACRA Championships

April 26, 2014

YPSILANTI, MI  – On Saturday, April 26th in Ypsilanti, Michigan, the University of Michigan Men's Rowing Team hosted and raced at the MACRA Championships. The regatta took place on scenic Ford Lake, and racing was delayed for a few hours due to high winds.

Once racing began, it opened with the Varsity Eight's heat, which they managed easily against Mercyhurst to qualify for the Grand Final. In the final, the 1V put down a blistering 5:51.3 to win the gold.

In the joint 2V and 3V event, the 2V has a commanding heat, beating Minnesota's 3V by open water and qualifying for the Grand Final. UM's 3V also qualified for the final by placing third in the other heat. In the Grand Final, the 2V beat Grand Valley by 5.6 seconds to take the gold, while the 3V placed fourth to win the 3V event.

The Freshman Four won their heat by over 30 seconds before mastering Cincinnati in the Grand Final to win. Inspired by their novice brethren, the Freshman Eight quelled the competition in their heat before a barnburner in the Grand Final, where the freshmen bested Notre Dame by 0.6 seconds. The 2F placed third in their heat and did not qualify for the final.

In a close race, UM's Lightweight Four placed second in their heat to Kansas by only 0.6 seconds. In their second matchup with Kansas in the Grand Final, the Wolverines were able to overcome the Jayhawks by placing fourth.

The Varsity Four landed a spot in the Grand Final, which was the last race of the day at 7:19 PM. In a five - lane battle due to a scratch from Saint Thomas, the Wolverines took gold by a healthy margin to close out the day for Michigan.

At the end of the day, Michigan took home a gold in every heavyweight event they entered in, including the 1V, 2V, 3V, 1F, Freshman Four, and Varsity Four. The squad will return to Ann Arbor to train before leaving for the Dad Vail regatta in Philadelphia on May 8th.
