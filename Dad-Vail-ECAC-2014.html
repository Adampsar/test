MEDIA

Dad Vail & ECAC Championship

May 8, 2014

PHILADELPHIA, PA  – On Thursday, May 8, the University of Michigan Men's Rowing Team travelled to Philadelphia, Pennsylvania to begin a long weekend of racing at both the Dad Vail Regatta (May 9th and 10th) and ECACs (May 11th). Affectionately known as "The Double," this two - regatta weekend has been completed by the squad before, including last season.

Racing opened for the team with the Varsity Four. The crew, made up of Maddie Boisvert, Alex Brown, Ben Dirkse, Lucien Gerondeau, and Bharat Srinivasan, won their Friday heat easily to advance to semifinals. On Saturday morning, the Four placed third and did not qualify for finals. The crew was four seconds behind Colgate and six seconds behind winner Oklahoma City. Colgate went on to win the event, while Oklahoma City placed second in the final, making the result even more disappointing.

Meanwhile, the Freshman Four placed second in their heat to easily qualify for semifinals, where they again placed second to get into the Grand Final. In a tight race, the freshmen placed fourth, a little over a second off of bronze medalist Drexel. Michigan's final Four to race, the Lightweight Four, easily qualified for semis, where they were downed by a fourth place finish.

The 1F dominated their heat and placed second in the semifinal to eventual winner Jacksonville to qualify for the final, where the freshmen wrecked hometown favorite and reigning winner Drexel by over five seconds for the bronze medal. This was the second straight year the squad's Freshman 8 medalled at the Dad Vail.

The Pair's heat featured some close racing, with Zach Coburn and Aamod Dekhne eventually beating out Wentworth Institute of Technology for the final semifinal qualification spot in their heat. A tough semifinal against Saint Thomas, Mercyhurst, and the College of Charleston left the Pair in fourth place and out of the Grand Final.

The 2V cruised to victory in their heat over Bucknell before defeating perennial rival Virginia in the semifinal to nab Lane 3 for the Grand Final. However, despite Michigan winning the 2V event at the Dad Vail in both 2012 and 2013, difficult conditions and a fast Drexel crew bested UM by over three seconds, giving the Wolverines a disappointing silver medal.

The Varsity 8 won their heat and semifinal comfortably before a dramatic Grand Final against 2013 gold medalist Drexel. In beautiful conditions, the crew of Demi Shanahan, Nick Sferrazza, Matt Herbers, Alex Crawford, Brandt Rosentretor, Wes Vear, Louis Schaljo, Mitch Tyson, and Philippe Kirschen rowed to victory, with Florida Institute of Technology placing second and Virginia finishing third. The win marked the fourth gold for the Varsity 8 in Michigan's last five Dad Vail appearances (2009, 2011, 2012, and 2014, with the Varsity 8 not attending in 2010), and secured the Men's Team Points Trophy for the Wolverines.

After three exhausting days of travel and racing, the team boarded a bus on late Saturday afternoon and drove from Philadelphia to Worcester, Massachusetts, where they would race at ECACs on Sunday. The squad did not arrive in Massachusetts until the wee hours of Sunday morning, giving them a significant disadvantage against their well - rested and fresh competitors.

On Sunday morning, the 2V held off Worcester Polytechnic Institute to qualify for the final. That afternoon, Williams grabbed an early lead from the Wolverines before the 2V pushed in the second half of the race to win by only a few seats, 6:00.323 to 6:01.017, with Hobart finishing third.

The 3V's dominance in their heat acted as a prelude for their final, in which the crew took an overwhelming six second victory over Hobart. Michigan's 4V, entered in the 3V event, qualified for the Petite Final, where they placed fifth.

UM's Open Four, comprised entirely of freshmen, controlled their heat before winning gold over Bucknell in the final. Meanwhile, the Four's fellow frosh in the 1F and 2F raced in an unusual 7 - lane straight final. Williams edged out the freshmen by slightly over a second, 6:14.440 to 6:15.609, while the 2F placed sixth.

In the 1V's heat, the Varsity 8 held off Bates to qualify for the final. That afternoon, the crew grabbed an early lead against Hobart before a blistering sprint gave Hobart the gold by less than half a second.

With gold medals in the 2V, 3V, and Open 4, as well as silvers in the 1V and 1F, UM was the clear winner for the Men's Points Trophy, wrapping up a long and successful weekend of racing. The Wolverines will return to Michigan to train before leaving for the ACRA National Championship in Gainesville, Georgia on May 22.
