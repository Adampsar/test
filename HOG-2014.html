MEDIA


HEAD OF THE GRAND

October 12, 2014

LANSING, MI  – On Sunday, October 12, the University of Michigan Men's Rowing Team made the short trip to Lansing, Michigan to race at the Head of the Grand in the squad's first regatta of the year. For many of the freshmen, the Grand was their first regatta ever. The race also acted as an important tune - up for the two crews headed to Boston next week for the Head of the Charles.

The first race of the day for UM was the Men's Collegiate 2-. Michigan entered five crews, using every pair the team owns. The A entry, stroked by recent Pairs Matrix champion Matt Herbers, won easily, with the team's other four entries finishing third, eighth, ninth, and eleventh in the field of 15.

The squad's early morning success in small boats continued in the Men's Collegiate 1X, where UM's two entries placed first and second. Freshman Lakin Vitton won with a time of 13:40.2, with Junior Jim Garay finishing only 8.6 seconds behind him in 13:48.8.

For the rest of the freshmen, their racing commenced with the Men's Collegiate Novice 8 race, where they placed third (behind Grand Valley and host Michigan State) and sixth in the field of 13. In the other flight of the Novice 8 event, Michigan's 3F, 4F, and 5F finished second, third, and fourth. The frosh also boated a 4, placing third in the Men's Collegiate Novice 4.

The Men's Collegiate Varsity 4 featured a crew comprised of four of the rowers from the Head of the Charles 8 in Ian McGraw, Alika Ziker, Dan Vitas, and Mitchell Tyson. The event also featured the 4 racing at the Charles, made up of Lucien Gerondeau, Ben Rebertus, Ben Getz, and Jim Garay. The 4s placed second and third, respectively, while UM's other entries finished seventh, ninth, and tenth.

In the final event of the day for the squad, the Men's Collegiate Varsity 8 featured five entries from Michigan. The crews placed first, third, fifth, seventh, and eleventh. The A entry finished in a time of 10:05.91, 16.4 seconds faster than second place Grand Valley.

The Grand marked a successful first regatta for the squad. The team will continue to train in Ann Arbor before 17 athletes (an 8, a 4, and three spares) travel to Boston to compete in the Head of the Charles next weekend.
