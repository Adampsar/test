MEDIA


Fours Matrix

November 15, 2014

ANN ARBOR, MI  – On Saturday, November 15, the varsity squad of the University of Michigan Men's Rowing Team competed in an intra - squad Fours Matrix. The Matrix acted as selection for the Wolverbuck Dual with Ohio State on November 22.

Each varsity member listed three preferred partners from the opposing side. Using this data, Coach Hartsuff created 18 pairings, which were then ranked based on an anonymous poll of participants. From these rankings, three heats of six pairs were made for Friday racing, with progression into a Third - Level Final, a Petite Final, and a Grand Final on Saturday on the line.

The format of the heats was five 900 meters pieces, run in a time trial format in Coxed Fours. After each piece, each pair switches into a different combination with another pair. After the five pieces, the total time of each pair determines the winners.

Racing began Friday morning at 7:30 AM. Seniors Daniel Vitas and Matt Herbers, ranked second, advanced to the Grand Final, along with Ian McGraw / Alika Ziker and Grey Braybrooks / Ben Rebertus. Advancing to the Petite Final were Jack Jasper / Toby Buth, Michael Griffin / Garrett Klumpar, and Ben Sonnega / Ben Dirkse.

Two heats were run simultaneously on Friday afternoon, and were highlighted by two dominant performances. With only one spot in the Petite Final available, Steve Sikora / Andrew Barber defeated Tucker Jameson / Hayden Sonnier by 8.4 seconds to advance. Jameson / Sonnier, as well as third place finishers Ben Getz / Colin Darnton and fourth place finishers Evan Harris / Jacob Dennis, moved on to the Third - Level Final.

The concurrent heat featured the biggest margin of the matrix, with top - seeded Alex Brown / William Burstein defeating third - seeded Wes Vear / Mitchell Tyson by over 12 seconds. In addition to the top two pairs, Lucien Gerondeau / Damian Rotarov advanced to the Grand Final while Jordan Shore / Scott Griffth, Jake Presto / Jim Garay, and William Denman / Philip Faris filled out the spots in the Petite Final.

With the finalists determined, the stage was set for close racing on Saturday morning. The Third - Level and Petite Finals were again run concurrently. After a delayed start due to equipment issues caused by the below freezing temperatures, tenth - ranked Getz / Darnton won the Third - Level Final, defeating Jameson / Sonnier and Denman / Faris, as well as Jordan Sykes / Zachary Dowling, Harris / Dennis, and Levi Godin (Freshman) / Ryan Breen, in that order.

In one of the closest Matrix finals of all time, Sikora / Barber and Griffin / Klumpar tied for first in the Petite Final, separated by only 1.2 seconds overall (under two seconds is technically a tie). Sonnega / Dirkse, Jasper / Buth, and Presto / Garay finished in a three - way tie for third, with only a one second difference between the three pairs. Sophomores Shore / Griffith finished sixth.

The tight racing continued in the Grand Final. After the first piece, Vitas / Herbers and McGraw / Ziker held a slim lead over Brown / Burstein and Gerondeau / Rotarov, who were only 0.8 seconds ahead of Vear / Tyson and Braybrooks / Rebertus. Despite Vitas / Herbers and Vear / Tyson finishing in 2:59.1 on the second piece (the fastest time of the Matrix), Brown / Burstein held a 2.2 second lead entering the penultimate piece. More hard - fought racing resulted in another tie, with Vitas / Herbers and Brown / Burstein separated by only 0.1 seconds overall and both pairs being crowned the victors. The same thin margin also produced a tie for third place between McGraw / Ziker and Vear / Tyson. Braybrooks / Rebertus finished only 2.7 seconds behind third place, and Gerondeau / Rotarov only 3.8 seconds behind them.

The Wolverines will continue training in Ann Arbor before the annual Wolverbuck Dual on Saturday, November 22, after which the team will move indoors full - time until spring break.
