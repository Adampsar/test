Michigan Men's Rowing Welcomes Class of 2019

September 26, 2015

ANN ARBOR, MI  – With the two - part selection process completed, the University of Michigan Men's Rowing Team has officially announced the current freshman class. 38 oarsmen and five coxswains were selected, the vast majority having never rowed before coming to UM.

Members of the Class of 2019 hail from 12 different states across the country, including California, New York, Illinois, Virginia, Ohio, Maryland, Massachusetts, Wisconsin, Tennessee, Iowa, New Jersey, and Michigan. The class also has an international presence, with oarsmen coming from as far as India and France.

The group also adds some height to the team, with 17 oarsmen standing 6'2 or taller. A sizeable portion of the class rowed in high school, with athletes coming from programs like Saint John's Jesuit (OH) and New Trier (IL), as well as local programs like Forest Hills Northern and Skyline.

Regarding the squad, Freshman Coach Russell Giacobbe said: "We are very excited about this new group. Our on - campus recruiting efforts netted us some talented walk - ons, who will be joined by a large group of high school rowers. We look forward to further developing the group over the next few months as we push toward our goal of winning the ACRA National Championship in late May."

The freshman will continue training in Ann Arbor before their inaugural competition next month.
